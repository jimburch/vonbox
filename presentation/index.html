Vonbox

An NFC-reading movie player

Jim Burch

01 · Why

What I wanted to build for Von.

My 3.5-year-old can't read or navigate Plex. Disney+ and YouTube put him in front of an endless feed. I wanted to give him a different way to start a movie.

  • 01
    Physical media. A real object you hold and tap. Not a UI.
  • 02
    Curated choices. A small library, hand-picked. No algorithm.
  • 03
    Kid autonomy. He starts movies himself. No "Dad, can you…"

Inspired by the Tonie Box  ·  simplyexplained.com's NFC movie library

Section 02

How it works.

Demo

Tapping a Blu-ray case from a cold start.

The flow

From tap to movie, end-to-end.

NTAG215 tap on box NFC sticker on a Blu-ray case
▼
Pico polls PN532 (10Hz) → debounce → publish vonbox/nfc/tapped  ·  {"uid": "…"}
▼
MQTT broker (Mosquitto on Synology)
▼
Home Assistant automation UID → rating key  ·  unscrobble  ·  wake TV  ·  play_media (deep link)
▼
Apple TV launches Plex → movie plays
▼
HA publishes vonbox/state → Pico renders LED + buzzer
Everything past the Pico happens in Home Assistant. The Pico only publishes the tap.
Message formats

MQTT payloads and the Plex deep link.

MQTT topics & payloads

vonbox/nfc/tapped  →  {"uid": "045AED5ECD2A81"}

vonbox/state       →  {"state": "loading", "title": "Mario Galaxy", ...}
vonbox/state       →  {"state": "playing", "title": "Mario Galaxy", ...}

The Plex deep link HA actually calls

plex://play/?metadataKey=/library/metadata/<key>&metadataType=1&server=<machine_id>
Adding a new movie is one line in a YAML map in Home Assistant. No firmware change on the Pico.
Section 03

Parts & software.

Hardware

Why a Pico 2 W instead of a Pi Zero W.

The Pi Zero W is the obvious reach — same family, Python everywhere. Three reasons I picked the Pico 2 W:

Power draw

~50 mA vs. ~400 mA

About 10× longer on a battery. Matters if the box is eventually cordless.

Boot time

Milliseconds vs. ~20 s

The Pico is ready immediately. A Pi Zero needs to boot Linux before responding.

Storage

Flash, not SD card

SD cards are the most common failure mode in hobby Pi projects. The Pico writes to onboard flash.

Tradeoff: dev loop is slower — flash and reboot, instead of editing Python over SSH.
Software

Why Home Assistant, and why MQTT.

Why Home Assistant

Reuse existing integrations.

HA already has Apple TV (via pyatv) and Plex integrations, plus webhooks, automations, and state tracking. Changing a movie mapping is a YAML edit, not a firmware flash.

Why MQTT, not HTTP

Bidirectional, persistent, lightweight.

The Pico publishes taps; HA publishes state back to drive the LED ring and buzzer. The broker handles reconnect. umqtt.simple on the Pico is a small dependency.

Section 04

Dev process.

Phase 1

I started with no hardware at all.

Before ordering parts, before any Pico code, the first thing I built was a single curl command: 

$ curl -X POST http://192.168.0.122:8123/api/webhook/play_toy_story_test

That webhook wakes the TV and soundbar over CEC, opens Plex on the Apple TV, and starts Toy Story.

The Apple TV + Plex + HA path was the highest-risk part of the project. I wanted to confirm it worked before ordering hardware.

By the time the parts arrived, the integration side was already proven.
Plan vs. reality

What I planned, and what actually shipped.

📋 Original plan
  • 3D-printed figurines
  • Continuous presence (place=play, remove=pause)
  • Volume knob + replay button on box
  • OLED display
  • 1-hour resume-window timer
🚀 v1 shipped
  • Blu-ray cases with NFC tags
  • Tap-and-go (tap fires once)
  • Siri Remote does it
  • LED ring + buzzer only
  • Always start fresh (Plex unscrobble)
The biggest change was the interaction model — moving from continuous presence (Tonie-style) to a one-shot tap.

Section 06

Biggest challenges.

Most of the difficulty was in the Home Assistant integration, not the hardware or the Pico code.

Challenge 01

tvOS 26.5 broke select_source.

Setup
The standard HA approach to open Plex on Apple TV is media_player.select_source('Plex').
Symptom
After the tvOS 26.5 update, select_source returned success but did nothing. The TV woke up, the Apple TV home screen appeared, and Plex never opened.
Diagnosis
tvOS 26.5 broke the Apple TV Companion app-list. HA's source list came back empty, so the call had nothing to match. Tracked in home-assistant/core#171666.
Fix
Bypass select_source and call play_media directly with a Plex deep link: 
plex://play/?metadataKey=/library/metadata/<key>&server=<id>
There are three vendors in the playback chain — Apple, Plex, and HA — and an update on any of them can break the automation.
Challenge 02

Features I planned for v1, then cut.

  • Volume knob (KY-040 rotary encoder)
    The Siri Remote already controls volume over CEC. No need to duplicate it. ADR 0002
  • Play / pause button
    Also handled by the Siri Remote. ADR 0002
  • OLED display
    Von can't read yet. The LED ring and buzzer cover the feedback the user needs. ADR 0003
  • Continuous presence (Tonie-style)
    RF detuning from a nearby hand made detection unreliable. Tap-and-go was simpler and a better fit for the use case. ADR 0001
Each cut is documented in an ADR. Most are returning for v2 once v1 is in regular use.
Challenge 03

Plex's resume position overrode the start.

Symptom
Tapping the Mario card resumed the movie wherever it had last stopped instead of starting from the beginning. Not what a 3-year-old expects.
Diagnosis
Plex tracks resume position server-side. play_media honors it by default, and the integration doesn't expose a flag to override.
Fix
Add a rest_command that calls Plex's /:/unscrobble endpoint before play_media runs. That clears the resume state for the movie. About six lines of YAML.
Most of the time on this one was spent finding the unscrobble endpoint, not writing the fix.
Smaller issues

Other things that took longer than expected.

  • iPhone Personal Hotspot defaults to 5 GHz. The Pico 2 W's CYW43 radio is 2.4 GHz only, so it would never associate. Enabling "Maximize Compatibility" in iOS settings forces 2.4 GHz.
  • umqtt.simple isn't frozen into the official Pico 2 W MicroPython build. A one-time mip install writes it to flash.
  • The LED ring at full white draws ~1.4 A, more than VSYS/USB can supply. Brightness is hard-capped at 25% in firmware as a safety limit.
  • The PN532 ships configured for UART. Two DIP switches on the back of the board need to be flipped for I²C — not obvious from the documentation.
